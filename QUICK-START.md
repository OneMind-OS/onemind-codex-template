# Quick Start — Your First 30 Minutes

> You don't need to understand the whole system. You need 4 buckets and 30 minutes.

---

## The Problem You're Solving

Your life is scattered across 15 apps, 3 notebooks, and your memory. Ideas get lost. Decisions get forgotten. Goals drift. And nothing talks to each other.

We're going to fix that. In 30 minutes, you'll have **one place for everything** — and it'll work for the rest of your life.

---

## Step 1 — Understand the Four Buckets (2 minutes)

Everything in your life fits into four buckets:

```
🧠  TECH & AI       Your tools, your systems, your digital infrastructure
💪  YOU              Your health, your money, your enterprise, your identity
🏡  YOUR FOUNDATION       Your relationships, community, home
👑  YOUR ENTERPRISE      Your career, ventures, brand, impact
```

That's it. Four buckets. When something comes into your life, you ask **one question: which bucket?**

| What happened | Which bucket? |
|--------------|---------------|
| Doctor's appointment | 💪 YOU |
| New career idea | 👑 YOUR ENTERPRISE |
| Community event invite | 🏡 YOUR FOUNDATION |
| Want to try a new AI tool | 🧠 TECH |
| Need to fix the fence | 🏡 YOUR FOUNDATION |
| Found a great article about investing | 💪 YOU (your money) |
| Client wants a proposal | 👑 YOUR ENTERPRISE |

**Quick exercise:** Think of 3 things on your mind right now. Put each in a bucket. Done? You just learned the One Mind organization system.

## Step 2 — Create Your First Notes (10 minutes)

Open your vault in Obsidian (or any text editor). You'll see four folders:

```
00-24 SO (Sovereign Operations)/    ← 🧠 TECH & AI
25-49 HP (Holistic Performance)/    ← 💪 YOU
50-74 LE (Legacy Evolution)/        ← 🏡 YOUR FOUNDATION
75-99 GE (Generational Enterprise)/ ← 👑 YOUR ENTERPRISE
```

**Create 3 notes.** Pick the templates that match what's on your mind:

Save it in `25-49 HP/` (the YOU bucket — it's about your health).

**Congratulations.** You just did the first two steps of CODEX: you **Captured** (got things out of your head) and **Organized** (put them in the right bucket with the right type).

## Step 3 — Learn the Five Steps (3 minutes)

The system has 5 steps. Here they are in plain English:

```
1. CAPTURE      Get it out of your head and into the vault
2. ORGANIZE     Put it in the right bucket
3. DIRECT       Decide what matters most and who's doing it
4. EXECUTE      Do the work (or let your AI help)
5. × MULTIPLY   Review your week — each review makes the system smarter
```

You just did steps 1 and 2.

**Step 3 (Direct)** is deciding what to work on. Look at your goals and tasks — which one matters most this week? That's your priority.

**Step 4 (Execute)** is doing the work. Go run. Make the call. Write the draft. If you have an AI agent, it can handle research, drafting, and analysis for you.

**Step 5 (× Multiply)** is your Sunday review. 30 minutes to look at what happened and plan next week. We call it "multiply" because every time you review, the system gets smarter. Your notes get cleaner. Your priorities get sharper. It compounds.

**That's the whole system.** Capture → Organize → Direct → Execute → × Multiply. Repeat weekly.

---

## Step 4 — Your Inbox (2 minutes)

Real life is messy. Sometimes you capture something and don't know which bucket it belongs in. That's fine.

**Every bucket has an inbox folder** called `06 Inbox (Queue)/`. When in doubt:

1. Drop it in the inbox of the **closest** bucket
2. If you can't even pick a bucket, drop it in the TECH inbox (it's the catch-all)
3. During your Sunday review, you'll sort the inbox — takes 5 minutes

**The golden rule:**

> **If it takes more than 10 seconds to decide where something goes, put it in the inbox and move on. Sort it Sunday.**

Never let the perfect folder stop you from writing something down.

---

## Step 5 — Your First Sunday Review (10 minutes)

This is the × (multiply) step. Do this every Sunday for 30 minutes:

### The Sunday Review Checklist

```
□  Open each inbox. Move things to the right place or delete them.
□  Look at your goals. Any progress this week?
□  Look at your tasks. What got done? What didn't?
□  What's the #1 priority for next week?
□  Write 3 sentences:
   - What worked this week
   - What didn't work
   - What I'll change next week
```

Save your review in the YOU bucket using the review template. That's it.

**Why this matters:** The review is where the magic happens. Each week you do this, you're not just organizing — you're teaching yourself (and your AI) what matters. The system gets smarter every cycle. That's the multiplication effect.

## What Comes Next

You now have a working CODEX. Four buckets, a few notes, and a weekly review. That's all you need to start.

As your system grows, here's what unlocks naturally:

### Month 1 — Domains Appear

Your buckets will naturally split into specific areas:

```
💪 YOU → Health, Finance, Identity
👑 YOUR ENTERPRISE → Career, Brand, Strategy, Ventures
🏡 YOUR FOUNDATION → Relationships, Community, Home
🧠 TECH → Agents, Tools, Automation
```

These are called **domains**. Each bucket can hold up to 25 domains. You activate them as you need them — most people use 15-30 total.

### Month 2 — More Note Types

Beyond goals, tasks, and notes, you'll discover:

| Template | When You Need It |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Decision** | Recording a choice with context so future-you remembers why |
| **Project** | Planning a multi-step effort with milestones |
| **Metric** | Tracking a number over time (weight, revenue, savings) |
| **SOP** | Documenting a repeatable process step-by-step |

### Month 3+ — AI Agents

If you're using AI agents (Claude, GPT, etc.), the vault becomes their context. Every note you write with good frontmatter teaches your AI about your life. Eventually, agents can:
- Triage your inbox
- Draft documents
- Research topics
- Track your metrics
- Run your SOPs

This is what makes One Mind an **operating system** instead of a notebook.
